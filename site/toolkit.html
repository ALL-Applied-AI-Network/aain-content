01 Day 1

Stand up your chapter.

Sign up for the dashboard at dashboard.all-ai-network.org. Your chapter has a live URL the moment you submit the form, and the dashboard becomes your operational core for everything that happens next.

Events & QR check-in

Create events with a single form. Each one gets a printable QR code; members scan to check in and earn points. Check-in history feeds the chapter leaderboard automatically.

Members + living resumes

Members opt in to a profile that pulls from GitHub + LinkedIn, plus their event/project history at your chapter. One click generates an AI-summarized “living resume” sponsors can browse.

Sponsor inbox & proposals

Every sponsor inquiry — from your hub site form or the sponsor portal — lands in your dashboard inbox. Accept or decline proposals; emails go out automatically.

Reports + exports

Attendance heatmaps, semester reports, sponsor ROI summaries. One-click export for advisor reports, year-end officer handoffs, and grant applications.

Hub-site sync

One API key from the dashboard wires your hub site to your real data: events, members, leaderboard, projects. Zero duplicated data entry.

Badges + points

Custom badges per chapter (“ROSIE Award winner”, “Hackathon champion”) plus a points system you can wire to whatever rewards make sense at your school.

You inherit four years of operational tooling on day one. No spreadsheets, no hand-rolled QR check-in, no DIY Stripe wiring. And every improvement we ship next quarter rolls out to your chapter automatically.

02 Week 1

Ship your chapter’s website.

Fork the hub-site template, edit one config file, push to deploy. Your chapter has a polished public website that auto-pulls curriculum, playbooks, and workshops from the shared content library — the “What you get” list below is read live from aain-hub-template.

1

Use the template

One click on GitHub — you get a full repo with dark theme, event pages, leaderboards, and sponsor showcase.

2

Edit hub.config.json

Set chapter name, university, colors, officers, social links. One file. Everything on the site updates from it.

3

Push to deploy

GitHub Actions deploys to GitHub Pages on push. Optional: we’ll wire your-hub.all-ai-network.org as a custom subdomain.

04 By year one

Get sponsored.

The hardest part of running a chapter is the financial side. The network handles the front of the funnel — companies sign up, browse chapters, find you automatically — while your eboard owns the relationship.

A

Sponsors find you

Companies sign up at sponsors.all-ai-network.org and browse chapters. They see your real data: members, recent events, tech focus, opt-in talent. No outreach required from you.

B

You review proposals

Tier + amount + message land in dashboard.all-ai-network.org/sponsors → Proposals. Accept, decline, or counter with a note — the sponsor gets emailed automatically.

C

Money goes to the chapter

100% of any sponsorship dollars go to the chapter directly. The network takes zero cut — we charge sponsors a small access subscription instead. No transaction fees, no escrow.

New to this? The Sponsors playbook walks through how MAIC landed its first sponsor (Direct Supply, $5K) and grew from there — read it before you cold-email anyone. Already have sponsors? Send them sponsors.all-ai-network.org to onboard onto the platform.
