# Post-Lab Follow-Through

What happens after the showcase determines whether your Innovation Lab is a one-off event or the start of a recurring program. This page covers sponsor debriefs, student feedback, publishing results, and building toward the next Lab.

## The Sponsor Debrief

Schedule a 30-minute debrief with your sponsor within one week of the showcase. This meeting is essential for maintaining the relationship.

### What to Cover
1. **Results summary**: Which teams produced the strongest work? Were any approaches surprising?
2. **What worked**: What aspects of the Lab format did the sponsor find most valuable?
3. **What to improve**: Where did communication break down? Was the problem scoped correctly? Did mentors have enough time?
4. **Talent follow-up**: Which students stood out? Does the sponsor want introductions for internships or full-time roles?
5. **Next steps for the work**: Will any team's solution be taken further internally?
6. **Future partnership**: Would the sponsor be interested in running another Lab next semester?

### The Debrief Report

Send a written summary within one week. Use this template:

```markdown
# Innovation Lab Debrief Report
## [Semester] [Year] - [Sponsor Name]

### Overview
- Problem: [one sentence]
- Duration: [X weeks]
- Teams: [X teams, Y total students]
- Showcase date: [date]

### Results Summary
| Team | Approach | Key Metric | Notable Findings |
|------|----------|-----------|-----------------|
| Team Alpha | XGBoost + feature engineering | 87% accuracy | Discovered time-of-day was strongest predictor |
| Team Beta | LSTM on sequence data | 82% accuracy | Novel approach to handling missing values |
| ... | ... | ... | ... |

### Winning Team
[Team name] won with [brief description of approach and results].

### Participation Metrics
- Student hours invested: ~[X] total
- Office hours sessions: [X]
- Milestone completion rate: [X]%

### Student Feedback Highlights
- [2-3 key quotes from student surveys, anonymized]

### Recommendations for Next Time
- [2-3 specific suggestions based on what you observed]

### Sponsor Satisfaction
- [Notes from the debrief conversation]

### Contact
[Your name, email, phone]
```

## Collecting Student Feedback

Send a post-Lab survey within 48 hours of the showcase, while the experience is fresh.

### Survey Questions

**Quantitative (1-5 scale)**:
- Overall Innovation Lab experience
- Quality of the problem statement
- Sponsor mentor availability and helpfulness
- Team dynamics and collaboration
- Organizer communication and support
- Likelihood of participating again
- Likelihood of recommending to a friend

**Open-ended**:
- What was the most valuable thing you learned?
- What was the biggest challenge you faced?
- What would you change about the format?
- How did this compare to other tech events you have attended?
- Any other feedback?

**Career-related**:
- Did this experience change your interest in [the problem domain]? (yes/no/maybe)
- Would you be interested in an internship or role at [sponsor company]? (yes/no/maybe)
- Can we share your resume with the sponsor? (yes/no)

### What to Do with the Feedback
- **Aggregate quantitative scores** and compare across semesters to track improvement
- **Identify recurring themes** in open-ended responses
- **Share relevant quotes** (with permission) in your debrief report and marketing materials
- **Act on the top 2-3 criticisms** before the next Lab

## Publishing Results

Sharing Lab results serves three purposes: it gives students portfolio content, it demonstrates your club's impact to future sponsors, and it attracts new members.

### What to Publish

**Club website or blog**:
- Event recap with photos (500-800 words)
- Problem description and winning team's approach (with sponsor permission)
- Participation statistics and student testimonials

**Social media** (LinkedIn, Twitter/X):
- Photo carousel from the showcase
- Tag the sponsor company and individual mentors
- Highlight winning team and their approach
- Use relevant hashtags and tag your university

**GitHub**:
- If the sponsor agrees, publish winning team's code (with data removed or anonymized)
- Include a clear README explaining the problem, approach, and results
- This becomes a portfolio piece for students and a proof point for your club

### Getting Sponsor Permission
Before publishing anything, confirm with the sponsor:
- [ ] Can we name the company in our recap?
- [ ] Can we describe the problem publicly?
- [ ] Can we share specific results or metrics?
- [ ] Can we publish team code?
- [ ] Are there any restrictions on what students can put in their portfolios?

Most sponsors will say yes to everything except publishing raw data. Get this in writing (an email confirmation is fine).

## Turning One Lab into a Recurring Program

### The Semester After
If the sponsor wants to continue:
- Schedule a planning meeting 6-8 weeks before the next Lab starts
- Discuss whether to repeat the same problem (deeper exploration) or tackle a new one
- Invite the winning team to serve as mentors for the next cohort

If the sponsor does not want to continue:
- Thank them graciously. Ask if they would provide a testimonial for future sponsor outreach.
- Start prospecting new sponsors using the results from this Lab as your proof point
- The debrief report and student testimonials are your best sales tools

### Building Institutional Knowledge
After each Lab, update your internal documentation:
- What went well and what to change (specific and actionable)
- Timeline with actual dates (not just the template)
- Budget breakdown (what you spent and where)
- Contact list of everyone involved
- File location for all assets (slides, rubrics, problem briefs)

Store this in a shared drive or Notion workspace that your successor can access. Club leadership turns over. Your documentation is what makes the program survive.

### Scaling Up
Once you have run 2-3 successful Labs:
- **Add a second sponsor per semester** (run Labs in sequence, not in parallel, unless you have a large organizing team)
- **Create a "Lab alumni" network** of past participants who can mentor future teams
- **Pitch larger sponsorships** using your track record (include stats: teams, students, projects, hiring outcomes)
- **Share your model with other hubs** in the ALL Applied AI Network so they can run their own Labs

## Key Metrics to Track Across Labs

| Metric | What It Tells You |
|--------|-------------------|
| Number of applicants vs. spots | Demand for the program |
| Milestone completion rate | How well teams stayed on track |
| Student satisfaction (1-5) | Quality of the experience |
| Sponsor satisfaction (1-5) | Quality of the partnership |
| Sponsor return rate | Whether sponsors find it valuable enough to repeat |
| Hiring outcomes | Students who got interviews or offers through the Lab |
| Public outputs | Blog posts, GitHub repos, or other portfolio content produced |

Track these semester over semester. They tell the story of your program's growth and impact.
