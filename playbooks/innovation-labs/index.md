# Innovation Labs: Sponsor-Backed AI Competitions

**We invented the Innovation Lab format in 2024 to give industry sponsors a way to work with students on real AI problems — not toy datasets. Brady Corp was our first partner, $5K prize pool, 60 students. It worked.**

## How we did it at MAIC

Innovation Labs are what happens when a sponsor brings us a real business problem, we give them access to 60+ motivated undergrads, and everyone ships something over a 2-month window. Unlike Hacksgiving (48 hours, nonprofit, AI-for-good) and Research Groups (6 months, original research, publication track), Innovation Labs sit in the middle: 8 weeks, industry-sponsored, optimized for a working prototype the sponsor can actually use.

The inaugural Innovation Lab ran October 10 – December 7, 2024 with **Brady Corporation**. Their Director of AI Operations, Franz, brought us a challenge: *predict the volume of liquid in a container from a single image.* Real use case — Brady's customers measure fluids in lab and industrial settings, and the current methods are slow. We gave students a YOLOv8 segmentation baseline and 8 weeks. 60 students from MSOE and UW-Milwaukee split into teams. Liquid Boogaloo won ($2.5K, 9 members). Liquid Lens took second ($1.5K). UW-Milwaukee took third ($1K). Every participant got a recommendation letter and a Brady Corp T-shirt.

Ben Paulson and Adam Haile co-organized the inaugural Lab. We've since run a Spring 2025 Innovation Lab with a different sponsor. The format works: sponsors get early access to talent, students get a portfolio project with a real company name on it, and we get a funded track that complements Hacksgiving.

## What we'd do differently

We under-scoped the NDA conversation. Brady's CEO and president were nervous about competitors seeing the code, and we spent two weeks in legal back-and-forth in September 2024 that we could have resolved earlier. Have the IP/NDA conversation in your **first** sponsor meeting, not your third. Lock the answer before students see the problem brief.

## Prerequisites

Don't run an Innovation Lab as your first event. You need:

- An active club with 20+ members who can commit to an 8-week project
- At least one successful Hacksgiving or similar event under your belt
- A warm intro to an industry sponsor willing to put up $5K+ and dedicate an engineer as mentor

Read [Getting Started](../getting-started/index.md) first if you're earlier in the arc.
