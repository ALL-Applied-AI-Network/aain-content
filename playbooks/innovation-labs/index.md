# Innovation Labs: Sponsor-Backed AI Competitions

Innovation Labs are structured, sponsor-backed competitions where student teams solve real industry problems using AI. They are the highest-impact event format in the ALL Applied AI Network playbook. At MSOE alone, Innovation Labs have engaged 300+ students and produced projects that sponsors have taken into production consideration.

Unlike hackathons (which optimize for speed and creativity), Innovation Labs run over multiple weeks with defined deliverables, mentor access, and judging criteria aligned to real business outcomes. Think of them as a compressed consulting engagement where students are the consultants and the sponsor provides the problem, data, and domain expertise.

## Who This Is For

A hub leader who has at least one industry sponsor ready to engage. You should have a functioning club with 20+ active members and experience running at least a few events. Innovation Labs require meaningful coordination, but the payoff for your members and your sponsor relationships is enormous.

## What Makes Innovation Labs Different

- **Real problems**: Sponsors bring actual business challenges, not toy datasets
- **Multi-week format**: Teams have time to iterate, not just hack together a demo
- **Mentor access**: Sponsor engineers and data scientists are available throughout
- **Tangible outcomes**: Final presentations to sponsor leadership with real feedback
- **Resume-grade experience**: Students walk away with portfolio projects that matter

## Quick Numbers

| Metric | Typical Range |
|---|---|
| Duration | 3-6 weeks |
| Team size | 3-5 students |
| Number of teams | 4-12 |
| Sponsor commitment | 10-20 hours total |
| Student time investment | 5-10 hours/week |

Make sure you have read the [Getting Started](../getting-started/index.md) playbook first. Innovation Labs build on the organizational foundations covered there. You will need a functioning leadership team, a room booking process, and at least one sponsor conversation in progress.
