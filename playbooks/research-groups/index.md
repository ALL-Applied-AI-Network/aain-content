# Research Groups: From Curiosity to Publication

**Research groups are how MAIC produces real AI work — not just consumes it. 21 groups, 142 students, 20+ papers, $10K+ in competition winnings over three years.**

## How we did it at MAIC

Research groups at MAIC are 6-month projects, November through April, that take a team of 3–8 students from literature review to a paper, poster, or conference submission. Every group gets a mentor (faculty, grad student, or industry researcher), access to ROSIE (our NVIDIA GPU-powered on-campus supercomputer, administered via Dr. Retert), and a path to present at MICS (the regional Midwest computing symposium) or the ROSIE Supercomputer Super Challenge.

In 2024 alone we ran 21 research groups with 142 students — the largest single-year cohort in club history. Sample projects from that year: **Lucas Gral — LLMs for Foreign Languages** (adaptive language learning that beats Duolingo-style flashcards); **William Lassiter — AI for Vocal Cord Mobility Analysis** (non-invasive ultrasound alternative to endoscopy); **Sam Keyser — Multi-Agent RL in "The Crew"** (cooperative agents with imperfect information, with applications to self-driving); **Ben Paulson — Nilus Project** (collaboration with a Chilean startup building AI for artificial glaciers and clean-water delivery in dry regions). Other named groups: NourishNet, Silent Sound Synthesizers, Brain Alignment Innovators.

Ben earned a Rosie Supercomputer Super Challenge finalist spot three years running and won MICS Best Paper in both 2022 and 2024. Research groups have won over $10,000 in AI-research competitions over the last three years and published more than 20 papers. The research track is what converts members into contributors.

## Incentives we created to make research groups sticky

| Incentive | How to earn |
|---|---|
| **Points** (toward merch) | Attending research group meetings |
| **Researcher Hoodie** | Participating in a research group |
| **MICS cord + stole** at graduation | Presenting research at MICS |
| **Publication credit** | Co-authoring a paper with the group |
| **ROSIE compute access** | Automatic for all research group members |

## What we'd do differently

Early research groups (2021–22) lost momentum around week 6 because we didn't set concrete milestones. Now we require a deliverable every 4 weeks: literature summary → experiment plan → first results → draft paper. If a group misses a milestone, we intervene with the mentor rather than letting it drift. The 2022 groups that hit their deliverables still produce citations in our 2024 papers — the ones that didn't, disappeared.

## Prerequisites

Research groups are not a first-semester event. You need:

- A club with 30+ active members, at least 5 of whom want depth over breadth
- Access to compute (GPU cluster, cloud credits, or a supercomputer like ROSIE)
- At least one faculty or industry mentor per group

If you're not there yet, start with Workshops and Speaker Events. Read [Getting Started](../getting-started/index.md) first.
