# Semester Planning Template

This is a 15-week semester plan for a research group. Adapt the dates to your academic calendar. The plan assumes weekly 90-minute meetings and 2-3 hours of individual work between meetings.

## The 15-Week Plan

### Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-3)

**Goal**: Form the group, choose a direction, and build shared knowledge.

#### Week 1: Kickoff
- [ ] Hold first meeting: introductions, expectations, logistics
- [ ] Assign roles (Project Lead, Literature Reviewer, Experiment Lead, Writer)
- [ ] Set up infrastructure: group chat, shared drive, paper repository, GitHub repo
- [ ] Each member identifies 2-3 research topics of interest

**Meeting agenda**: Introductions, overview of semester plan, interest inventory, logistics setup

#### Week 2: Topic Exploration
- [ ] Each member reads 1-2 survey/overview papers on candidate topics
- [ ] Members post reading notes to shared document before meeting
- [ ] Group discussion: compare topics on feasibility, interest, and available resources
- [ ] Narrow to 2-3 candidate directions

**Meeting agenda**: Paper discussions (15 min each), feasibility assessment, topic narrowing

#### Week 3: Direction Committed
- [ ] Final topic decision made
- [ ] Research question drafted (using the template from Choosing a Direction)
- [ ] Initial reading list of 6-8 papers created
- [ ] First paper assigned for detailed reading next week
- [ ] Reach out to potential faculty mentor

**Meeting agenda**: Topic vote, research question workshop, reading list creation, mentor outreach plan

---

### Phase 2: Deep Reading (Weeks 4-6)

**Goal**: Build expertise in your chosen area and identify where your contribution fits.

#### Week 4: Paper Reading Begins
- [ ] First paper presented and discussed (full reading protocol)
- [ ] All members submit reading notes before meeting
- [ ] Identify 2-3 key questions raised by the paper
- [ ] Begin concept map of the research landscape

**Meeting agenda**: Paper presentation (20 min), discussion (30 min), connections to our question (15 min), planning (15 min)

#### Week 5: Building Depth
- [ ] Second paper presented and discussed
- [ ] Update concept map with new connections
- [ ] Begin exploring available datasets and code repositories
- [ ] First mentor meeting (if mentor secured)

**Meeting agenda**: Paper presentation and discussion, dataset/code exploration report, mentor meeting debrief

#### Week 6: Transition Planning
- [ ] Third paper presented and discussed
- [ ] Draft experiment plan: what will we test, what data will we use, what tools do we need
- [ ] Set up development environment (everyone can run baseline code)
- [ ] Finalize the experiment tracking system (W&B, spreadsheet, or MLflow)

**Meeting agenda**: Paper discussion, experiment plan workshop, technical setup check

**Milestone check**: By end of Week 6, the group should be able to answer:
- What is our research question?
- What has been done before?
- What will we do differently?
- What data and tools will we use?

---

### Phase 3: Experimentation (Weeks 7-11)

**Goal**: Run experiments, iterate, and produce results.

#### Week 7: Baseline Reproduction
- [ ] Reproduce baseline results from a key paper (or establish your own baseline)
- [ ] Verify evaluation pipeline works correctly
- [ ] Document the baseline setup for reproducibility
- [ ] Continue reading 0-1 papers per week as needed

**Meeting agenda**: Baseline results review, evaluation pipeline check, experiment queue planning

#### Week 8: First Experiments
- [ ] Run first set of experiments testing your approach
- [ ] Record all results in tracking system
- [ ] Begin writing the Related Work section of your output
- [ ] Analyze initial results and plan next experiments

**Meeting agenda**: Results review, analysis discussion, writing check-in, next experiment decisions

#### Week 9: Iteration
- [ ] Run second round of experiments based on Week 8 findings
- [ ] Conduct error analysis on model failures
- [ ] Begin writing the Method section
- [ ] Mid-semester check: are we on track for our target output?

**Meeting agenda**: Results review, error analysis deep dive, mid-semester assessment

#### Week 10: Deepening
- [ ] Run ablation studies or additional comparisons
- [ ] Continue writing Method and begin Experiments section
- [ ] Prepare mid-project presentation for mentor or club
- [ ] Decide on final experiments needed

**Meeting agenda**: Ablation results, writing progress review, presentation prep

#### Week 11: Final Experiments
- [ ] Complete all remaining experiments
- [ ] Generate final results tables and figures
- [ ] Finalize the Experiments and Results sections
- [ ] All experimental code cleaned up and documented

**Meeting agenda**: Final results review, figure workshop, code cleanup assignments

**Milestone check**: By end of Week 11, the group should have:
- Complete experimental results
- Draft of Method, Related Work, and Experiments sections
- Clean, reproducible code
- Key figures and tables generated

---

### Phase 4: Writing and Presentation (Weeks 12-15)

**Goal**: Produce the final output and share it.

#### Week 12: Writing Sprint
- [ ] Draft Introduction and Discussion/Analysis sections
- [ ] All sections under review by at least one other group member
- [ ] Internal editing session: read sections aloud, identify gaps
- [ ] Send draft to mentor for feedback

**Meeting agenda**: Full-group editing session, gap identification, mentor feedback plan

#### Week 13: Revision
- [ ] Incorporate mentor feedback
- [ ] Write Abstract and Conclusion
- [ ] Complete second draft of full paper/blog post/report
- [ ] Begin preparing presentation or poster
- [ ] If submitting to a venue, check formatting requirements

**Meeting agenda**: Revision review, presentation planning, submission logistics

#### Week 14: Polish and Submit
- [ ] Final proofreading pass (every member reads the complete output)
- [ ] Format according to venue requirements (if submitting)
- [ ] Submit paper or publish blog post
- [ ] Finalize presentation slides or poster
- [ ] Practice presentation at least twice

**Meeting agenda**: Final review, submission, presentation rehearsal

#### Week 15: Present and Reflect
- [ ] Present at club meeting, university symposium, or other venue
- [ ] Publish code repository (clean README, requirements, instructions)
- [ ] Group retrospective: what worked, what to improve
- [ ] Document lessons learned for next semester's group
- [ ] Celebrate

**Meeting agenda**: Presentation, retrospective discussion, handoff documentation

## Adjusting the Plan

This template assumes everything goes smoothly. It will not. Common adjustments:

- **Topic pivot (Week 4-5)**: If reading reveals your original question is not feasible, pivot early. Lose 1-2 weeks to save the whole semester.
- **Slower reading phase**: If your topic requires heavy background, extend Phase 2 by 1-2 weeks and compress Phase 4.
- **Faster experiment phase**: If your experiments are quick (benchmarking existing tools), you may finish Phase 3 early. Use the extra time for deeper analysis or additional experiments.
- **No publication target**: If your group decides a blog post or open-source tool is the right output, shift writing time toward documentation, tutorials, and code quality.

The plan is a tool, not a contract. Update it as you learn more about your project and your group's pace.
