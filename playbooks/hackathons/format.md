# Deciding Your Format

Before you book a room or contact a sponsor, nail down the format. Every other decision flows from this one. The wrong format for your club's size and experience will create unnecessary stress and a worse experience for participants.

## Duration Options

### 12-Hour Sprint (Recommended for First-Timers)
- **Schedule:** Saturday 8 AM - 8 PM (or similar single-day block)
- **Best for:** Club jams (15-30 people), first-time organizers
- **Pros:** No overnight logistics, simpler food planning, less burnout
- **Cons:** Less time for ambitious projects, tighter build window
- **Tip:** A 12-hour hackathon with high energy beats a 24-hour hackathon where everyone is exhausted by hour 16.

### 24-Hour Classic
- **Schedule:** Saturday noon to Sunday noon (the most common format)
- **Best for:** Mid-size events (50-100 people), experienced organizers
- **Pros:** Enough time for real prototypes, the "classic" hackathon feel
- **Cons:** Need overnight venue access, sleeping arrangements, more food runs
- **Tip:** Plan for 30% of participants to leave overnight and return in the morning. This is normal.

### 48-Hour Weekend
- **Schedule:** Friday evening to Sunday afternoon
- **Best for:** Large campus-wide events (150+), events with complex challenge tracks
- **Pros:** Maximum build time, allows for iteration
- **Cons:** Significant logistics overhead, participant fatigue is real
- **Tip:** Only attempt this if you have 10+ organizers and previous hackathon experience.

## Location Format

### In-Person Only
- Best for building community and collaboration
- Requires a venue with sufficient power, Wi-Fi, and seating
- Mentors can circulate naturally
- **Choose this if:** Your campus has good event spaces and most participants are local

### Hybrid (In-Person + Virtual)
- Expands reach but splits your attention
- Requires a solid streaming and communication setup (Discord is essential)
- Virtual participants often feel like second-class citizens unless you design for them intentionally
- **Choose this if:** You want to include participants from partner schools or remote members

### Fully Virtual
- Lowest logistics overhead
- Hardest to maintain energy and collaboration
- Works best with strong Discord moderation and scheduled check-ins
- **Choose this if:** Your club is distributed or you have no venue access

## Theme vs. Open-Ended

### Themed Hackathon
- Examples: "AI for Accessibility," "Healthcare Data Challenge," "Campus Problems"
- **Pros:** Easier to attract sponsors (they can align with the theme), more focused projects, better for beginners who struggle with open-ended ideation
- **Cons:** May exclude participants with niche interests
- **Recommendation:** Use a broad theme with 2-3 specific challenge tracks underneath

### Open-Ended
- Participants build whatever they want
- **Pros:** Maximum creative freedom, attracts experienced hackers
- **Cons:** Harder to judge fairly, projects vary wildly in scope
- **Recommendation:** Better for internal club jams where the goal is learning, not competition

### Competitive vs. Collaborative
- **Competitive:** Teams compete for prizes, judged by rubric. Higher energy, more polish.
- **Collaborative:** Focus on learning and building, minimal prizes. Lower pressure, more experimentation.
- **Hybrid approach:** Award prizes but also celebrate "most creative," "best learning moment," and "best pivot" to reward the process, not just the output.

## Decision Checklist

Use this to lock in your format before moving to the next phase:

- [ ] Duration chosen: _____ hours
- [ ] Location format: in-person / hybrid / virtual
- [ ] Theme decided: _____ (or open-ended)
- [ ] Competitive or collaborative (or hybrid)
- [ ] Expected participant count: _____
- [ ] Minimum organizer team size confirmed (see Scale Guide in index)
- [ ] Format shared with full organizing team for alignment

## Common Mistakes

1. **Going too big too fast.** Your first hackathon should be a 12-hour club jam. Prove the concept, then scale.
2. **Picking a 48-hour format without the team to support it.** Overnight events need people awake at 3 AM.
3. **Running hybrid without dedicated virtual support.** If you cannot assign someone to manage the virtual experience full-time, go in-person only.
4. **Choosing a theme that is too narrow.** "AI for predicting Wisconsin dairy yields" will attract five people. "AI for food systems" will attract fifty.
