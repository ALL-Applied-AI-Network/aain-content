# Sponsorship and Budget

Sponsors make the difference between pizza-and-water and an event people remember. But sponsorship is a relationship, not a transaction. Approach it that way and you will have an easier time getting support and keeping sponsors for future events.

## What Sponsors Want

Before you ask for money, understand what you are offering in return:

- **Recruiting access:** This is the number one reason companies sponsor student hackathons. They want to meet talented students.
- **Brand visibility:** Logo placement, mentions in social posts, demo day presence.
- **Product adoption:** Some companies sponsor to get students using their platform (cloud credits, API access, developer tools).
- **Community goodwill:** Especially for local companies, supporting student events is good PR.

## Sponsorship Tiers

A simple three-tier structure works well for most hackathons:

| Tier | Amount | What They Get |
|------|--------|---------------|
| Gold | $1,000-2,500 | Logo on all materials, 5-min opening ceremony slot, dedicated recruiting table, social media features, named challenge track (optional) |
| Silver | $500-1,000 | Logo on materials, mention in opening ceremony, social media mention |
| Bronze | $100-500 | Logo on website and event signage |
| In-Kind | Varies | Listed as supporter (for companies providing food, prizes, cloud credits, etc.) |

**Tip:** Adjust amounts based on your region. $500 from a local startup is equivalent to $2,000 from a tech giant in terms of effort to secure.

## Finding Sponsors

Start with the warmest leads and work outward:

1. **Your university's career services or industry partnerships office.** They often have contacts and sometimes funding.
2. **Companies that already recruit on your campus.** They have budget allocated for student engagement.
3. **Local tech companies and startups.** Smaller companies are often more responsive and flexible.
4. **Cloud and developer tool providers.** AWS, Google Cloud, Azure, and others have educational sponsorship programs.
5. **Alumni network.** Alumni working at tech companies can champion your event internally.
6. **ALL Applied AI Network partners.** Check if there are network-level sponsorships you can tap into.

## Outreach Email Template

Send this to a specific person (not "info@company.com"). Find engineering managers, developer advocates, or campus recruiting leads on LinkedIn.

> **Subject:** Sponsorship opportunity: [Event Name] AI Hackathon at [University]
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> I am [Your Name], president of [Club Name] at [University]. We are hosting [Event Name], a [duration] hackathon focused on [theme] on [date]. We expect [number] students from [departments/schools].
>
> We would love to have [Company] involved as a sponsor. Our Gold tier ($X) includes [top 2 benefits]. I have attached our sponsorship packet with full details.
>
> Would you be open to a 15-minute call this week to discuss? I am happy to work around your schedule.
>
> Best,
> [Your Name]

**Follow-up cadence:** If no response after 5 business days, send a polite follow-up. After two follow-ups with no response, move on.

## Budget Breakdown

### Small Club Jam (20-30 people, $200-500)

| Item | Cost |
|------|------|
| Food (pizza, snacks, drinks) | $150-300 |
| Prizes (gift cards, small items) | $50-150 |
| Supplies (name tags, markers) | $20-50 |

### Mid-Size Event (50-100 people, $1,000-3,000)

| Item | Cost |
|------|------|
| Food (catered meals, snacks, coffee) | $600-1,500 |
| Prizes | $200-500 |
| T-shirts or swag | $200-500 |
| Supplies and signage | $50-150 |
| A/V equipment rental (if needed) | $100-300 |

### Large Campus-Wide (150+ people, $5,000-15,000)

| Item | Cost |
|------|------|
| Food (full catering, multiple meals) | $3,000-7,000 |
| Prizes | $500-2,000 |
| Swag (shirts, stickers, bags) | $1,000-2,500 |
| Venue rental (if applicable) | $500-2,000 |
| A/V and streaming | $200-500 |
| Marketing and printing | $100-300 |

## Running on a Minimal Budget

No sponsors yet? You can still run a great event:

- **Use free campus rooms.** Most universities have reservable event spaces at no cost.
- **Ask your student government for funding.** Many allocate money for student org events.
- **In-kind sponsorship counts.** A local pizzeria donating food is worth more than its dollar value.
- **Skip swag.** Nobody needs another t-shirt. Put the money into food and prizes.
- **Use free tools.** Discord (communication), Devpost (submissions), Canva (design), Google Forms (registration).

## Sponsor Management Checklist

- [ ] Create a shared spreadsheet tracking all sponsor outreach (company, contact, status, amount, tier)
- [ ] Send sponsorship packets at least 6 weeks before the event
- [ ] Confirm sponsor logos and preferred company name spelling
- [ ] Send sponsors a pre-event brief: schedule, their specific benefits, point of contact
- [ ] Assign an organizer to greet each on-site sponsor
- [ ] Send thank-you emails within 48 hours of the event with photos and attendance numbers
- [ ] Ask if they would like to sponsor next semester's event (plant the seed early)

## Tax and Financial Notes

- If your club is part of a university student org, funds may need to go through your school's finance office.
- Keep all receipts. Your treasurer will thank you.
- Sponsor invoices should come from your university or fiscal sponsor, not personal accounts.
- If your hub is under the ALL Applied AI Network's 501(c)(3) umbrella, donations may be tax-deductible -- confirm with network leadership.
