# Day-Of Operations

Event day is when all your planning pays off. Your job as an organizer shifts from planning to execution. The goal is to make everything run smoothly so participants can focus entirely on building.

## Setup (2-3 Hours Before Start)

### Venue Setup Checklist
- [ ] Tables and chairs arranged (clusters of 4-6 for team seating)
- [ ] Power strips and extension cords distributed (one strip per table minimum)
- [ ] Wi-Fi network name and password posted visibly on multiple signs
- [ ] Projector and A/V tested with your laptop and at least one other device
- [ ] Registration/check-in table set up near the entrance
- [ ] Signage posted: directions, schedule, Wi-Fi info, mentor station, food area
- [ ] Food and drink area set up (separate from hacking area to reduce mess near laptops)
- [ ] Quiet zone designated (for teams that need focus time)
- [ ] Organizer supplies accessible: tape, markers, sticky notes, extra chargers, first aid kit

### Tech Setup
- [ ] Test Wi-Fi with 5+ devices simultaneously (if it buckles, have a backup plan)
- [ ] Verify the event Discord/Slack is active and announcement channel is ready
- [ ] Project submission platform is live and tested
- [ ] Streaming setup tested (if hybrid event)

## Opening Ceremony Script

Keep it under 15 minutes. Participants are eager to start building.

1. **Welcome (2 min):** Thank everyone for coming, thank sponsors by name, introduce the organizing team.
2. **What we are building today (3 min):** Describe the challenge tracks. Keep it high-level and inspiring.
3. **Logistics (3 min):** Schedule overview, food timing, Wi-Fi info, where to find mentors, how to submit projects.
4. **Rules and code of conduct (2 min):** Be respectful, collaborate, ask for help. Link to the full code of conduct document.
5. **Sponsor spotlight (3-5 min):** Give Gold sponsors 2-3 minutes each. Silver sponsors get a name mention. Keep this tight.
6. **"Go build" (1 min):** Officially start the hackathon. Make it energetic.

## Managing the Room

### Organizer Rotation
No single organizer should be "on" for the entire event. Set up shifts:
- Shift 1: Event start through dinner (6-7 hours)
- Shift 2: Evening through midnight (5-6 hours)
- Shift 3: Overnight through breakfast (6-7 hours, for 24h events)
- Shift 4: Morning through closing (5-6 hours)

Each shift should have at least 2 organizers present.

### Hourly Walk-Throughs
Every hour, one organizer should walk the room and:
- Check if any teams look stuck or frustrated (offer to connect them with a mentor)
- Refill water and snack stations
- Make sure power strips are not overloaded
- Check the help channel on Discord for unanswered questions
- Ensure the noise level is reasonable

### Announcements
Use both Discord and a brief in-person announcement for important updates:
- Food arriving
- Submission deadline reminders (3 hours, 1 hour, 30 minutes, 10 minutes)
- Schedule changes
- Mentor availability windows

## Food and Energy Management

### Timing Matters
- Serve lunch within the first 90 minutes. Hungry people do not code well.
- Dinner between 5:30-6:30 PM. A real meal, not just more pizza.
- Midnight snacks: energy bars, fruit, chips, candy. Keep it accessible all night.
- Breakfast: bagels, muffins, coffee (lots of coffee). People emerging from overnight hacking need carbs and caffeine.
- Have water available continuously. Dehydrated hackers get headaches.

### Budget Tips
- Pizza is fine for one meal. Do not serve pizza for every meal.
- Costco runs for snacks and drinks are your best friend. Budget $3-5 per person for snacks.
- Catering from local restaurants is usually cheaper per plate than you think. Get quotes.
- Always over-order by 10%. Running out of food is worse than having leftovers.

## Handling Common Problems

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Wi-Fi goes down | Have a mobile hotspot as backup. Contact campus IT emergency line. |
| Team member drops out | Help the remaining members rescope. Connect them with solo participants. |
| Team conflict | Organizer mediates. If unresolvable, help individuals find new teams. |
| Project is not working 2 hours before deadline | Encourage them to simplify. A working demo of a small feature beats a broken demo of a big idea. |
| Power outage | This is why you told people to save their work. Have flashlights. Call campus facilities. |
| Someone is sick or injured | First aid kit for minor issues. Call campus security or 911 for anything serious. Do not hesitate. |
| Participant is being disrespectful | Private conversation, refer to code of conduct. Remove if necessary. |

## Closing Ceremony

Keep it to 30 minutes:

1. Thank participants for their incredible work (2 min)
2. Finalist demos (if using two-round judging) (15 min)
3. Announce winners by category (5 min)
4. Thank sponsors, mentors, and organizing team (3 min)
5. Share what is next: how to continue projects, upcoming club events (2 min)
6. Group photo (1 min)

## Cleanup Checklist

- [ ] All trash collected and disposed of
- [ ] Tables and chairs returned to original positions
- [ ] All borrowed A/V equipment returned
- [ ] Lost and found items collected and posted about on Discord
- [ ] Venue inspected for any damage (report immediately if found)
- [ ] Thank the venue contact or building manager before leaving
