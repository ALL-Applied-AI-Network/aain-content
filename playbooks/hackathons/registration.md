# Registration and Team Formation

Good registration does three things: collects the information you need, sets expectations for participants, and helps you plan logistics. Bad registration either asks too many questions (people abandon it) or too few (you are guessing on food counts at the last minute).

## Registration Platform Options

| Platform | Cost | Best For |
|----------|------|----------|
| Luma | Free | Clean RSVP pages, good for smaller events |
| Eventbrite | Free under 25 tickets | Larger events, waitlist management |
| Google Forms | Free | Maximum flexibility, but no built-in waitlist |
| Devpost | Free | Hackathon-specific features, project submission built in |
| Custom form (Typeform, Tally) | Free tier available | Branded experience |

**Recommendation:** For your first hackathon, use Google Forms for registration and Devpost for project submission. Do not over-engineer this.

## What to Ask on the Registration Form

### Essential Fields
- [ ] Full name
- [ ] Email address (school email preferred)
- [ ] University and year (freshman, sophomore, etc.)
- [ ] Major or area of study
- [ ] Experience level with programming (beginner / intermediate / advanced)
- [ ] Dietary restrictions (vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, allergies)
- [ ] Do you have a team or want help finding one?
- [ ] T-shirt size (if providing swag)

### Optional but Useful
- [ ] What are you hoping to learn or build?
- [ ] Have you attended a hackathon before?
- [ ] Anything we should know to make the event accessible to you?
- [ ] How did you hear about this event? (helps measure marketing channels)

### Do Not Ask
- Government ID numbers, financial information, or anything unrelated to the event
- More than 12 questions total (completion rates drop sharply after that)

## Setting Capacity and Managing Waitlists

### Determining Capacity
- Venue capacity is your hard limit. Check fire code maximums.
- Plan for 60-70% of registrants to actually show up. If you want 80 attendees, accept 110-120 registrations.
- For a first event, cap at 30-40. You can always scale up.

### Waitlist Strategy
1. Set a registration cap slightly above your target (accounting for no-shows)
2. When the cap is hit, move new registrations to a waitlist
3. Send waitlisted people an email: "You're on the waitlist. We'll notify you by [date] if a spot opens."
4. One week before the event, send a confirmation email to all registrants: "Please confirm your attendance by [date] or your spot will be released."
5. Release unconfirmed spots to waitlisted participants

**Tip:** The confirmation email is the single most effective way to reduce no-shows. People who confirm are 3x more likely to attend.

## Team Formation

### Pre-Formed Teams
- Allow teams of 2-5 people (4 is the sweet spot)
- Let people register as a team or individually
- Require at least one team contact email for communication

### Day-Of Team Matching
Many participants will show up solo. Have a plan:

1. **Team-finding session (15-20 minutes after opening ceremony).** Have solo participants stand up, introduce themselves in 30 seconds: name, year, skills, what they want to build.
2. **Skill-based grouping.** Put colored stickers on name badges: green = frontend, blue = backend, red = ML/data, yellow = design. Encourage mixed-color teams.
3. **Discord team-finding channel.** Open this a few days before the event so people can start connecting early.
4. **"Looking for team" board.** A physical whiteboard where people can post sticky notes with their name, skills, and interests.

### Solo Participants
Some people prefer to work alone. That is fine. Make it an explicit option during team formation.

## Pre-Event Communication

### Confirmation Email (1 week before)
Send to all registrants:

> **Subject:** [Event Name] is in one week -- confirm your spot
>
> Hey [Name],
>
> [Event Name] is one week away! Here are the details:
>
> - **Date:** [Date and time]
> - **Location:** [Venue with link to map]
> - **What to bring:** Laptop, charger, water bottle, student ID
>
> **Please confirm your attendance** by replying to this email or clicking [link] by [deadline]. Unconfirmed spots will be released to our waitlist.
>
> Questions? Reply to this email or ask in our Discord: [link]

### Final Reminder (48 hours before)
- Reiterate schedule, location, and what to bring
- Include parking information if relevant
- Link to the Discord server
- Mention team formation for solo participants

## Registration Checklist

- [ ] Registration form created and tested (have 2-3 people try it)
- [ ] Capacity limit set based on venue and organizer bandwidth
- [ ] Waitlist process defined
- [ ] Confirmation email drafted and scheduled
- [ ] Final reminder email drafted and scheduled
- [ ] Registration data exported and shared with logistics lead (for food counts, badge printing)
- [ ] Team formation plan decided (pre-formed, day-of matching, or both)
- [ ] Discord/Slack server set up with team-finding channel
