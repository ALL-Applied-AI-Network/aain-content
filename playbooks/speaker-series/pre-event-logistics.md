# Pre-Event Logistics

A talk can have a brilliant speaker and still fall flat because the projector did not work, the room was too small, or nobody remembered to promote it. This section covers the operational details that make the difference between a smooth event and a scramble.

## Room Booking

### What to Look For
- **Capacity:** Book a room for 1.5x your expected attendance. A packed room feels energetic. A half-empty auditorium feels dead.
- **A/V equipment:** Projector or large screen, HDMI and USB-C adapters, speakers with microphone (for 40+ people)
- **Seating layout:** Theater-style or classroom-style works for talks. Avoid rooms with fixed lab benches.
- **Accessibility:** Ground floor or elevator access, wheelchair-friendly seating
- **Wi-Fi:** Essential if the speaker needs to demo anything live
- **Booking process:** Know your university's room reservation system. Popular rooms book weeks in advance.

### Booking Timeline
- **4+ weeks out:** Submit room reservation request
- **2 weeks out:** Confirm reservation, visit the room to check A/V
- **1 week out:** Send room details to speaker (building name, room number, parking info)
- **Day of:** Arrive 30 minutes early to set up

**Tip:** Build a relationship with the person who manages room bookings. Being polite and reliable means you get priority for good rooms.

## A/V Setup Checklist

- [ ] Projector or screen tested and working
- [ ] Correct adapters available (HDMI, USB-C to HDMI, DisplayPort -- bring all three)
- [ ] Speaker's laptop connects and displays correctly (test during prep call or 30 min before)
- [ ] Audio system tested (microphone, speakers) for rooms over 40 seats
- [ ] Backup plan: can the speaker present from your laptop if theirs fails?
- [ ] Slide advancer/clicker available (optional but appreciated by speakers)
- [ ] Recording equipment set up (if recording -- see [Recording and Sharing](./post-event.md))
- [ ] Zoom/virtual meeting set up and screen-sharing tested (if hybrid)

### Common A/V Failures and Fixes

| Problem | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| No signal from laptop | Try a different adapter. Switch HDMI ports. Restart the projector. |
| Audio feedback/echo | Move the microphone away from speakers. Lower speaker volume. |
| Zoom participants cannot hear | Use an external microphone pointed at the speaker, not the laptop mic. |
| Slides look washed out | Close blinds, dim lights near the screen. |
| Wi-Fi too slow for demo | Speaker uses phone hotspot as backup. Or pre-record the demo. |

## Speaker Prep Call

Schedule a 15-minute call 1-2 weeks before the event. Cover:

1. **Talk content:** What will they cover? Any slides to review?
2. **Audience context:** "Our audience is mostly [year] students studying [field]. They are comfortable with [topics] but newer to [topics]."
3. **Format confirmation:** Duration, Q&A expectations, whether you will moderate questions
4. **A/V needs:** Any special software, live demo requirements, presenter notes setup
5. **Recording consent:** Will the talk be recorded? Where will it be shared?
6. **Logistics:** Where to park, which building, who will meet them, what time to arrive

**Tip:** Send a follow-up email after the prep call summarizing everything discussed. Speakers juggle many commitments and appreciate having details in writing.

## Slide Collection

- Ask the speaker to share slides at least 48 hours before the event (so you can have them as a backup on your laptop)
- If the speaker is not using slides, that is fine -- fireside chats and AMAs often do not need them
- Do not require slides to be in a specific template. The speaker's content is what matters.

## Pre-Event Promotion Timeline

| When | Action |
|------|--------|
| 2 weeks before | Announce the event on all channels (social media, Discord, email) |
| 1 week before | Second promotional push with speaker bio and topic details |
| 3 days before | Reminder post on social media and Discord |
| Day before | Final reminder via email and Discord with all logistics |
| 1 hour before | "Starting in 1 hour" post on Discord and social media |

See [Promotion and Attendance](./promotion.md) for detailed promotion strategies.

## Day-Before Checklist

- [ ] Room reservation confirmed (check for last-minute changes)
- [ ] A/V equipment confirmed available
- [ ] Speaker has all logistics details (time, location, parking, your phone number)
- [ ] Promotion posts scheduled or sent
- [ ] Printed materials ready (if any): speaker intro notes, agenda
- [ ] Food or refreshments ordered (if providing -- even just coffee and cookies makes a difference)
- [ ] Name tent or introduction card prepared for the speaker
- [ ] Phone charged (you will need it for photos and coordination)
- [ ] Backup slides loaded on your laptop

## Food and Refreshments

You do not need to cater every talk, but offering something creates a better atmosphere:

| Option | Cost per Person | When to Use |
|--------|----------------|-------------|
| Nothing | $0 | Lunchtime talks where people bring their own food |
| Coffee and cookies | $2-3 | Standard evening talks |
| Pizza and drinks | $5-8 | Special events, first talk of the semester |
| Catered dinner with speaker | $10-15 | High-profile speakers, sponsor-funded events |

**Tip:** Put food at the back of the room so latecomers can grab a plate without walking in front of the speaker.

## Emergency Contacts

Keep these on your phone for event day:
- Campus security / non-emergency line
- Building facilities / maintenance
- IT help desk (for A/V emergencies)
- Your club advisor's phone number
- The speaker's phone number
- At least one other organizer's phone number
