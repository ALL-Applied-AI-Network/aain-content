# Post-Event Follow-Up

The event is over, but the work that turns a single talk into lasting value happens in the next 48 hours. Good follow-up builds speaker relationships, creates content, and generates momentum for your next event.

## Within 24 Hours

### Thank the Speaker
Send a personal thank-you email (not a mass email -- a real one from the host).

> **Subject:** Thank you for speaking at [Club Name]
>
> Hi [Speaker Name],
>
> Thank you so much for your talk last night. Our members really enjoyed it -- the Q&A session was one of the most engaged we have had all semester.
>
> A few highlights:
> - We had [X] attendees (our best turnout this month)
> - Several members mentioned [specific thing they found valuable]
> - [Name] is planning to follow up with you about [topic discussed]
>
> We would love to stay in touch and potentially have you back in a future semester. I will also share the recording [if applicable] and social media posts once they are up.
>
> Thanks again for your time and generosity.
>
> Best,
> [Your Name]

**Tip:** Mention something specific from the talk. Generic thank-yous feel generic. A specific reference shows you were paying attention and that their time mattered.

### Thank Attendees
Post in Discord/Slack and send an email:

> Thanks to everyone who came out for [Speaker Name]'s talk on [topic]. If you missed it, [recording will be posted soon / here's a summary of key takeaways].
>
> Our next event is [date]: [brief description]. See you there.

## Recording Distribution

If you recorded the talk (with speaker consent), distribute it within one week.

### Recording Checklist
- [ ] Review the recording for quality (audio clear, slides visible)
- [ ] Trim the beginning and end (remove setup chatter and post-event noise)
- [ ] Add a title card or intro slide if possible (club name, speaker name, date)
- [ ] Upload to your preferred platform:
  - **YouTube (unlisted or public):** Best for discoverability and sharing
  - **Google Drive:** Simple, good for internal sharing
  - **Club website:** Builds your content library
- [ ] Share the link with the speaker for approval before publishing publicly
- [ ] Post the recording to Discord, email list, and social media

### Recording Best Practices
- Use OBS (free) for screen + camera recording on a laptop
- If in-person, place a camera or phone on a tripod at the back of the room
- Prioritize clear audio over video quality -- a talk with bad audio is unwatchable
- Record the screen separately from the room camera if possible (slides + speaker face)
- For Zoom talks, use Zoom's built-in recording (cloud recording is more reliable than local)

## Feedback Collection

Send a 3-5 question survey to attendees within 24 hours:

1. How would you rate tonight's talk? (1-5 stars)
2. What was the most valuable thing you learned?
3. What topics would you like to hear about in future talks?
4. Any suggestions for improving our events?
5. Know someone who would be a great speaker? (Name/contact info)

**Where to send:** Embed the survey link in your thank-you message on Discord and email. Keep it to under 2 minutes to complete.

**What to do with feedback:**
- Share ratings with the speaker (if positive) in your thank-you email
- Use topic suggestions to guide your speaker search for upcoming events
- Track ratings over time to see which formats and topics perform best
- Act on logistical feedback before the next event

## Social Media Recap

Post within 24-48 hours while the event is fresh.

### What to Include
- 1-2 photos from the event (speaker presenting, engaged audience)
- Speaker's name, role, and company (tag them)
- 2-3 key takeaways from the talk (in your own words, not direct quotes)
- A thank-you to the speaker
- A teaser for your next event

### Example LinkedIn Post

> Last night [Speaker Name] from [Company] joined [Club Name] at [University] to talk about [topic].
>
> Key takeaways:
> - [Insight 1 in your own words]
> - [Insight 2 in your own words]
> - [Insight 3 in your own words]
>
> Thanks [Speaker Name] for sharing your experience with our [X] members. Up next: [Next event teaser].
>
> [Photo]

**Tip:** Tag the speaker and their company. They often reshare, which exposes your club to their professional network.

## Converting Speakers into Ongoing Relationships

A single talk is valuable. An ongoing relationship is transformative. Here is how to nurture it:

### Immediate Follow-Ups
- Connect with the speaker on LinkedIn (all officers, not just the host)
- Add them to your speaker alumni list
- If they mentioned being open to mentoring, follow up with specific members who could benefit

### Long-Term Relationship Building
- Share the recording and social media post with them
- Send a brief update at the end of the semester: "Here's what our club accomplished this term" (shows their talk was part of something larger)
- Invite them to future events as an attendee or judge (not just a speaker)
- Ask if their company would be interested in sponsoring a hackathon or project
- Refer other hubs in the ALL Applied AI Network to them as a potential speaker

### The Referral Ask
In your thank-you email or a follow-up 1-2 weeks later:

> By the way, we are always looking for great speakers. Is there a colleague or someone in your network you think our members would love to hear from? A warm introduction goes a long way.

This single question can fill your speaker calendar for months.

## Post-Event Checklist

### Within 24 Hours
- [ ] Thank-you email sent to speaker
- [ ] Thank-you message posted for attendees
- [ ] Feedback survey sent
- [ ] Photos saved and backed up
- [ ] Attendance count recorded

### Within 1 Week
- [ ] Recording edited and uploaded (if applicable)
- [ ] Recording link shared with speaker for approval
- [ ] Social media recap posted
- [ ] Feedback survey results reviewed
- [ ] Speaker added to alumni tracker

### Within 2 Weeks
- [ ] Recording published to all channels
- [ ] Referral ask sent to speaker (if not included in thank-you)
- [ ] Lessons learned noted for future events
- [ ] Next event promotion started
