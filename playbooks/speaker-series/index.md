# Speaker Series: Bringing Industry to Campus

A well-run speaker series connects your members directly to practitioners who are building with AI every day. It is one of the lowest-effort, highest-value event formats you can run. A single compelling talk can shift a student's career trajectory, spark a research interest, or open a door to an internship.

This playbook covers everything from finding speakers to filling the room to making sure the Q&A does not die after two questions.

## Who This Is For

Any hub leader who wants to run recurring talks. Works well even for brand-new clubs since it requires minimal technical setup and no sponsor budget (most speakers will present for free if you ask well).

## Why Run a Speaker Series

- Low logistics overhead compared to workshops or competitions
- Builds your club's reputation with faculty and industry
- Gives members exposure to career paths and real-world AI applications
- Creates networking opportunities that workshops cannot
- Generates content (recordings, summaries) that promotes your club

## Recommended Cadence

For most clubs, one talk every two weeks during the academic term is sustainable and keeps momentum. Alternate speaker series weeks with hands-on workshop weeks for a balanced calendar.
